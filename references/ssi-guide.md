# SSI (Social Selling Index) Guide

LinkedIn's SSI score (0–100) is available at `linkedin.com/sales/ssi`. It measures four pillars, each scored 0–25. For job seekers, the score directly correlates with how often the profile surfaces in recruiter searches.

## How to collect SSI

**Own account (browser available):** Navigate to `linkedin.com/sales/ssi` in the user's active browser session and read all values directly.

**Own account (no browser session):** Ask the user to:
1. Open `linkedin.com/sales/ssi` while logged in
2. Take a screenshot of the full page
3. Upload it here

**Someone else's account:** The account holder must log in to their own LinkedIn, navigate to `linkedin.com/sales/ssi`, screenshot the full page, and share it.

Read from the screenshot or live page:
- Overall SSI score (out of 100)
- All four pillar scores (each out of 25)
- Industry rank % ("You rank in the top X% of your industry")
- Network rank % ("You rank in the top X% of your network")

---

## Score interpretation for job seekers

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 70–100 | Strong. Profile is actively surfaced in recruiter searches. Focus on quality of connections and content. |
| 50–69 | Moderate. Findable but losing to higher-SSI candidates. Targeted improvements will help. |
| 30–49 | Weak. Missing significant visibility. Multiple pillars need attention. |
| < 30 | Very weak. Essentially not in the running for passive recruiter discovery. |

Industry average is typically 20–30 for most professionals. Above 50 is competitive. Above 70 is strong.

## The Four Pillars

### 1. Establish Your Professional Brand (0–25)

What it measures: Profile completeness, content publishing, endorsements.

**For job seekers this means:**
- Complete profile (photo, headline, About, all Experience entries with descriptions, Skills 40+)
- Publishing at least occasional content (posts or articles)
- Getting endorsements on key skills

**Low score fixes:**
- Fill in all empty sections — the About section is the biggest lever
- Add 40+ relevant skills and get endorsements from connections
- Publish one substantive post or article (even a 200-word lesson learned from a recent project)

---

### 2. Find the Right People (0–25)

What it measures: How actively the user searches for and views profiles of relevant people.

**For job seekers this means:**
- Searching for hiring managers, recruiters, and people in roles they want
- Using LinkedIn search with filters (by company, title, location)
- Viewing profiles of people at target companies

**Low score fixes:**
- Search for "Recruiter" + your industry in LinkedIn search 3–4 times per week
- Browse profiles of people in your target role at target companies
- Use "People also viewed" to discover relevant connections

---

### 3. Engage with Insights (0–25)

What it measures: Engagement with posts and content — liking, commenting, sharing.

**For job seekers this means:**
- Commenting on posts from people in their industry (especially at target companies)
- Sharing relevant articles with brief personal commentary
- Reacting to posts from connections

**Low score fixes (high ROI, fast results):**
- Leave 2–3 substantive comments per week (15+ words, real insight — not "Great post!")
- Follow 5 top voices in your field and engage with their posts
- This is the fastest pillar to improve with low time investment

---

### 4. Build Relationships (0–25)

What it measures: Acceptance rate of connection requests, connections to decision-makers, message response rate.

**For job seekers this means:**
- Sending personalized connection requests to recruiters and people in their field
- Building connections at target companies before applying
- Responding to messages promptly (response rate affects this score)

**Low score fixes:**
- Send 5–10 personalized connection requests per week; always add a note
- Connect with every recruiter who views the profile
- Respond to all messages within 24–48 hours, even to decline

---

## SSI and recruiter visibility

LinkedIn's recruiter tool ranks candidates partly by SSI. A candidate with a 65 SSI will appear higher in search results than an identical profile with a 35 SSI. For job seekers who can't control how many people apply to a role, SSI is one of the few levers they can actively pull to improve passive discoverability.

**Fastest SSI improvements for most people:**
1. Fill in the About section and add 40+ skills → lifts Pillar 1 quickly
2. Comment 2–3×/week → lifts Pillar 3 within 2 weeks
3. Send 10 personalized connection requests to people at target companies → lifts Pillar 4
