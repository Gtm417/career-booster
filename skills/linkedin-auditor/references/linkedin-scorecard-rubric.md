# LinkedIn Scorecard Rubric

## Section weights

| Section | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | 20% | First text recruiter sees; primary keyword field for LinkedIn search ranking |
| About | 15% | Second most-read section; narrative and keyword density |
| Experience — current role | 15% | Proof of claim; ATS and recruiter read this first |
| Experience — prior roles | 10% | Career arc and progression signal |
| Skills | 10% | Directly drives LinkedIn search filters and endorsement signals |
| Recommendations | 10% | Social proof; affects SSI Build Relationships pillar |
| Featured | 5% | Portfolio and credibility signal; often empty |
| Education & Certs | 5% | Credential filter matching |
| URL & Contact | 5% | Friction reduction; custom URL improves brand |
| Photo & Banner | 3% | Trust and professionalism signal; affects click-through |
| Open to Work | 2% | Recruiter filter signal |
| **Total** | **100%** | |

---

## 1–5 scoring anchors

### Headline (weight 20%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Contains target job title, 1–2 high-value keywords, and a specific differentiator (industry, specialization, or result). Under 220 characters. |
| 4 | Contains target job title and keywords but differentiator is generic ("results-driven", "passionate"). |
| 3 | Contains job title but no keywords or vague/outdated title. |
| 2 | No job title — only company name, "open to work", or current employer role. |
| 1 | Empty, placeholder, or purely personal ("Dad | Dreamer"). |

### About (weight 15%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | 150–300 words. Opens with target role. Contains 5+ role-specific keywords naturally woven in. States value proposition and 1–2 notable achievements with numbers. Ends with a CTA or contact line. |
| 4 | Good length and keywords but either no numbers/achievements or no CTA. |
| 3 | Present but generic — reads like a job description or character summary. Few keywords. |
| 2 | Under 100 words or clearly written for a different role. |
| 1 | Empty. |

### Experience — current role (weight 15%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Title matches target role or clear progression toward it. 3–5 bullet points per role. At least 2 bullets have quantified impact (%, €/$, scale). Keywords from the target role appear naturally. Dates current. |
| 4 | Good bullets and keywords but quantification is weak or 1 bullet missing. |
| 3 | Bullets present but describe duties not achievements. No numbers. |
| 2 | Only a title and date — no description. |
| 1 | Missing or title only with clearly wrong role. |

### Experience — prior roles (weight 10%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | All roles have descriptions. Career arc shows clear progression. Each role has at least 1 quantified achievement. Older roles have shorter entries (1–2 bullets). |
| 4 | Most roles described; minor gaps in quantification. |
| 3 | Some roles described, others empty. Mixed quality. |
| 2 | Most roles title-only. |
| 1 | Empty or single title-only entry. |

### Skills (weight 10%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | 20+ skills listed. Top 3 pinned skills match the target role exactly. Skills include both hard (technical) and soft (contextual) entries. At least 5 skills have 5+ endorsements. |
| 4 | 15–20 skills, top 3 reasonably matched, endorsements present but sparse. |
| 3 | 10–15 skills, top 3 not pinned or mismatched. Few endorsements. |
| 2 | Under 10 skills or skills irrelevant to target role. |
| 1 | Empty or Skills section hidden. |

### Recommendations (weight 10%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | 5+ recommendations. At least 3 from managers or senior colleagues. Recommendations reference specific skills or projects relevant to the target role. |
| 4 | 3–4 recommendations of reasonable quality. |
| 3 | 1–2 recommendations, or all from peers only. |
| 2 | 1 recommendation, low quality or irrelevant. |
| 1 | No recommendations. |

### Featured (weight 5%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | 2–4 featured items. Includes portfolio, published work, case study, or notable project directly relevant to the target role. |
| 4 | 1–2 relevant featured items. |
| 3 | Featured section present but items are off-topic or low value (e.g., generic LinkedIn posts). |
| 2 | Featured section exists but empty or contains only a profile photo. |
| 1 | Featured section absent. |

### Education & Certs (weight 5%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Relevant degree listed with institution, dates, and field of study. Additional certifications present that are in-demand for the target role (with issuer and date). |
| 4 | Degree present, certifications present but not role-specific or outdated. |
| 3 | Degree listed, no certifications. |
| 2 | Incomplete education entry (no field of study or dates). No certs. |
| 1 | Empty or hidden. |

### URL & Contact (weight 5%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Custom LinkedIn URL (linkedin.com/in/firstname-lastname). Email visible in contact info. Optional: website or portfolio link. |
| 4 | Custom URL but no email or other contact info. |
| 3 | Default LinkedIn URL (contains random string). Contact info present. |
| 2 | Default URL, no contact info. |
| 1 | No contact info and no custom URL. |

### Photo & Banner (weight 3%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Professional headshot (clear face, good lighting, appropriate background). Custom banner relevant to the industry or role (not default blue). |
| 4 | Good headshot, default or generic banner. |
| 3 | Acceptable headshot (informal but not inappropriate), no banner. |
| 2 | Low quality, cropped from group photo, or avatar. |
| 1 | No photo. |

### Open to Work (weight 2%)

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 5 | Open to Work enabled and configured with correct target titles, locations, and job types. |
| 3 | Open to Work enabled but incomplete or misconfigured (wrong titles or location). |
| 1 | Not enabled (note: only flag if the user is actively job searching; otherwise N/A). |

---

## Weighted score calculation

```
overall = sum(section_score × section_weight) for all sections
```

Round to one decimal. Scale is 1.0–5.0.

**Benchmarks:**
- 4.5–5.0: Recruiter-optimized. Strong visibility.
- 3.5–4.4: Competitive. Minor improvements recommended.
- 2.5–3.4: Average. Significant improvements needed before active outreach.
- < 2.5: Weak. Run `/optimize-linkedin` before submitting any applications.
