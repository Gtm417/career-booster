# LinkedIn Scorecard Rubric

Used exclusively by `linkedin-auditor`. Score each section 1–5 using the anchors below. Overall profile score = weighted average, rounded to one decimal.

## Section weights

| Section | Weight | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | 20% | Highest search-ranking weight; appears in every recruiter interaction |
| About | 15% | Top of profile; determines whether recruiter keeps reading |
| Experience (current role) | 15% | Carries strong search weight beyond headline |
| Experience (prior roles) | 10% | Establishes trajectory and depth |
| Skills | 10% | Recruiters filter candidate searches on these |
| Photo & Banner | 8% | ~19% of recruiter evaluation time spent here |
| Featured | 7% | Strongest signal of substance beyond claims |
| Recommendations | 5% | Social proof |
| Education & Certifications | 4% | Completeness and relevance |
| Custom URL & Contact | 3% | Polish signal |
| Open to Work signaling | 3% | Direct recruiter discoverability |

---

## Scoring anchors

### Headline
- **1** — Just a job title ("Architect at Acme"). Or empty.
- **2** — Job title + company + one buzzword. No keywords a recruiter would search for.
- **3** — Includes role + a domain/industry keyword. Generic but findable.
- **4** — Clear identity + specific value or specialty + at least one credibility marker. Front-loads keywords.
- **5** — Pipe-separated formula: `[Identity] | [Specific value] | [Credibility] | [Secondary keywords]`. First 40 chars hook on mobile. Both acronyms and spelled-out terms present.

### About
- **1** — Empty, or one generic sentence.
- **2** — A few sentences; first-person fluff ("passionate about…"); no hook, no specifics, no CTA.
- **3** — Tells what they do, but reads like a resume summary. Few concrete results. No clear CTA.
- **4** — Strong hook in first 2 lines (visible before "see more"). Concrete results. Clear positioning. Soft CTA.
- **5** — Hook → narrative arc → quantified proof points → explicit value proposition → clear CTA. Keyword-dense without keyword-stuffing.

### Experience (current role)
- **1** — Title only, no description. Or bullets that say "Responsible for X."
- **2** — Description present but vague. No numbers. Reads as job description, not achievement.
- **3** — Mix of responsibilities and results. Some specifics. Title is the literal job title.
- **4** — Bullets lead with verbs, include outcomes, mention specific tools or methods. Title is keyword-optimized.
- **5** — Every bullet: action → context → quantified result. Specific tools and methodologies recruiters search for. Title is keyword-optimized (e.g. "Senior Architect | Sustainable Design | BIM" not just "Architect").

### Experience (prior roles)
- **1** — Most prior roles have no description.
- **2** — Some descriptions, all generic.
- **3** — Descriptions present but inconsistent depth.
- **4** — Each role has 2–4 bullets with results. Career trajectory is readable.
- **5** — Each role tells a chapter of a coherent story. Earlier roles are shorter but still show impact. Promotions and scope growth are visible.

### Skills
- **1** — Under 10 skills, or generic ones only (Microsoft Word, Teamwork).
- **2** — 10–25 skills, mix of relevant and noise.
- **3** — 25–50 mostly relevant skills, top 3 pinned reasonably.
- **4** — 40+ relevant skills, top 3 are exactly what recruiters search for in the target role.
- **5** — Near the 100-skill cap, all relevant, top 3 pinned strategically, endorsements from credible connections on key skills.

### Photo & Banner
- **1** — No photo, or selfie/group/casual photo.
- **2** — Professional-ish photo but poor framing, lighting, or background. Default LinkedIn banner.
- **3** — Decent headshot. Banner exists but is a generic stock image.
- **4** — Sharp professional headshot, eye contact, good crop. Custom banner with clear intent.
- **5** — Headshot follows best practice (face 60–70% of frame, eyes upper third, solid or neutral background). Banner reinforces positioning with tagline, social proof, or relevant visual.

### Featured
- **1** — Empty.
- **2** — One generic item (e.g. a company About page).
- **3** — 2–3 items, mixed quality.
- **4** — 3–5 strong items: a project, an article, a portfolio link. Each has a custom thumbnail and description.
- **5** — Curated 4–6 items that prove the positioning claimed elsewhere on the profile. Mix of formats. Each is a compelling next step for a recruiter.

### Recommendations
- **1** — None.
- **2** — One, generic.
- **3** — 2–3 recommendations.
- **4** — 3–5 substantive recommendations from credible sources (managers, peers, clients).
- **5** — 5+ recommendations spanning roles and perspectives; each speaks to specific strengths relevant to the target role.

### Education & Certifications
- **1** — Missing or incomplete.
- **2** — Education present, no certifications.
- **3** — Complete education; one or two certifications.
- **4** — Education + relevant certifications with verification links.
- **5** — All credentials current, industry-recognized, and directly relevant to the target role.

### Custom URL & Contact
- **1** — Default URL with random characters. No contact info beyond LinkedIn.
- **3** — Custom URL but no extra contact info.
- **5** — Custom URL using their real name. Professional email, relevant website, and up to 3 website links in Contact Info where appropriate.

### Open to Work signaling
- **1** — Not signaled anywhere even though actively job hunting.
- **3** — "Open to Work" turned on but with vague preferences.
- **5** — "Open to Work" set with specific roles, locations, and start date. If currently employed: visible to recruiters only (the green banner is more visible but controversial; recommend recruiter-only setting unless user explicitly wants the public banner).

## Overall score interpretation

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 4.5–5.0 | Recruiter-ready. Fine-tuning only. |
| 3.5–4.4 | Solid foundation. Targeted improvements will lift inbound meaningfully. |
| 2.5–3.4 | Average. Significant work needed before this profile competes for the target role. |
| 1.5–2.4 | Below the bar. Major rewrites required across multiple sections. |
| < 1.5 | Essentially unindexed by recruiters. Recommend full rewrite workflow. |
